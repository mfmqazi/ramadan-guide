Chapter 1

Introduction

Welcome to the Blessed Month

Ramadan is the ninth month of the Islamic lunar calendar and holds immense significance in the life of every Muslim. It is a month of fasting, prayer, reflection, and community. The month commemorates the first revelation of the Qur'an to Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him).

This sacred month is an opportunity for spiritual renewal, self-discipline, and drawing closer to Allah. Through fasting from dawn to sunset, Muslims develop empathy for those less fortunate and strengthen their connection with their Creator.

About This Guide

Dr. Farhat Hashmi's comprehensive work on Ramadan provides deep insights into the spiritual, practical, and social aspects of this blessed month. This guide covers:

  • The virtues and blessings of Ramadan
  • Detailed guidance on fasting and its spiritual dimensions
  • The special relationship between Ramadan and the Qur'an
  • Seeking Laylatul Qadr (The Night of Decree)
  • The practice of I'tikaf (spiritual retreat)
  • Zakat and charitable giving
  • Celebrating Eid with proper etiquette

Chapter 3

Fasting (Sawm)

The Obligation of Fasting

Fasting during Ramadan is one of the Five Pillars of Islam, obligatory upon every adult, sane Muslim who is physically able. Allah says in the Qur'an: "O you who believe! Fasting is prescribed for you as it was prescribed for those before you, that you may become righteous." (Qur'an 2:183)

Rules and Regulations

  • Fasting begins at dawn (Fajr) and ends at sunset (Maghrib)
  • Intention (niyyah) must be made before dawn for each day's fast
  • Abstinence from food, drink, and marital relations during fasting hours
  • Maintaining good character and avoiding sinful behavior
  • Suhoor (pre-dawn meal) is recommended and blessed
  • Breaking fast promptly at sunset is encouraged

Who is Exempt?

  • The sick who may be harmed by fasting
  • Travelers on long journeys
  • Pregnant and nursing women if fasting poses risk
  • Menstruating women (must make up days later)
  • The elderly who cannot physically fast
  • Those with chronic illnesses (may pay fidyah instead)

Spiritual Dimensions of Fasting

Fasting is not merely abstaining from food and drink; it's a comprehensive act of worship that trains the soul. It develops:

  • Taqwa (consciousness and fear of Allah)
  • Self-discipline and willpower
  • Empathy for the poor and hungry
  • Gratitude for Allah's blessings
  • Patience and perseverance
  • Control over desires and impulses

Chapter 4

The Qur'an in Ramadan

The Month of the Qur'an

Ramadan and the Qur'an share a special bond. Allah revealed the Qur'an during this blessed month, specifically on Laylatul Qadr. The Angel Jibreel would review the Qur'an with Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) every Ramadan, and in his final year, they reviewed it twice.

Recitation and Reflection

  • Recite the Qur'an daily, aiming to complete it during Ramadan
  • Understand the meanings through translation and tafsir
  • Reflect on the verses and apply them to your life
  • Listen to beautiful recitations to enhance connection
  • Memorize portions of the Qur'an
  • Attend Taraweeh prayers where the Qur'an is recited

Taraweeh Prayers

Taraweeh are special night prayers performed during Ramadan. The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "Whoever stands (in prayer) during Ramadan with faith and seeking reward, his previous sins will be forgiven." These prayers typically involve recitation of the entire Qur'an over the course of the month.

Living by the Qur'an

Ramadan is the perfect time to strengthen your relationship with the Qur'an. Beyond recitation, focus on:

  • Implementing Qur'anic teachings in daily life
  • Improving character based on Qur'anic guidance
  • Making the Qur'an a source of healing and comfort
  • Teaching the Qur'an to family members
  • Supporting Qur'an education initiatives

Chapter 5

Laylatul Qadr - The Night of Decree

The Most Blessed Night

Laylatul Qadr is the night when the Qur'an was first revealed. Allah describes it as better than a thousand months (over 83 years). Worship on this single night surpasses worship for a thousand months. It is a night of peace, blessings, and divine mercy.

"Indeed, We sent it down during the Night of Decree. And what can make you know what is the Night of Decree? The Night of Decree is better than a thousand months. The angels and the Spirit descend therein by permission of their Lord for every matter. Peace it is until the emergence of dawn." (Qur'an 97:1-5)

When is Laylatul Qadr?

The exact date of Laylatul Qadr is not specified, but it falls within the last ten nights of Ramadan, particularly on the odd nights (21st, 23rd, 25th, 27th, or 29th). The Prophet (peace be upon him) encouraged seeking it during these nights, with the 27th night being commonly emphasized.

How to Seek Laylatul Qadr

  • Perform extra prayers (Qiyam al-Layl) throughout the night
  • Recite the Qur'an extensively
  • Make abundant supplications and seek forgiveness
  • Engage in dhikr (remembrance of Allah)
  • Give charity generously
  • Perform I'tikaf in the last ten days
  • Recite the special dua: "Allahumma innaka 'afuwwun tuhibbul 'afwa fa'fu 'anni" (O Allah, You are Forgiving and love forgiveness, so forgive me)

Signs of Laylatul Qadr

  • A sense of peace and tranquility in the heart
  • The night is neither hot nor cold
  • The sun rises the next morning without strong rays
  • A feeling of spiritual elevation and closeness to Allah

Chapter 6

I'tikaf - Spiritual Retreat

What is I'tikaf?

I'tikaf is a spiritual retreat in the mosque, where a person secludes themselves for worship, particularly during the last ten days of Ramadan. The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) regularly performed I'tikaf during these days to seek Laylatul Qadr and dedicate himself entirely to worship.

Purpose and Benefits

  • Complete devotion to worship without worldly distractions
  • Seeking Laylatul Qadr with focused dedication
  • Strengthening connection with Allah through continuous worship
  • Detachment from material concerns and worldly affairs
  • Spiritual purification and self-reflection
  • Following the Sunnah of the Prophet (peace be upon him)

Rules and Guidelines

  • Must be performed in a mosque
  • Typically done during the last ten days of Ramadan
  • Person should not leave the mosque except for necessary reasons
  • Focus on prayer, Qur'an recitation, and dhikr
  • Avoid unnecessary conversation and worldly activities
  • Can be performed by both men and women (women in designated areas)
  • Minimum duration varies by scholarly opinion, but often one day

Activities During I'tikaf

  • Performing the five daily prayers and Taraweeh
  • Extensive Qur'an recitation and study
  • Making sincere supplications
  • Engaging in dhikr and glorification of Allah
  • Seeking knowledge through Islamic books
  • Reflecting on one's life and seeking repentance

Chapter 7

Zakat and Sadaqah

The Obligation of Zakat

Zakat is one of the Five Pillars of Islam, an obligatory charity that purifies wealth and helps those in need. While Zakat is due annually when wealth reaches a certain threshold (nisab), many Muslims choose to pay it during Ramadan to maximize blessings.

Who Must Pay Zakat?

  • Muslims who possess wealth above the nisab threshold
  • The wealth must be owned for one lunar year
  • The person must be of sound mind and adult
  • Zakat is typically 2.5% of accumulated wealth

Recipients of Zakat

The Qur'an specifies eight categories of people eligible to receive Zakat:

  • The poor (al-fuqara)
  • The needy (al-masakin)
  • Zakat administrators
  • Those whose hearts are to be reconciled
  • Those in bondage (slaves and captives)
  • Those in debt
  • In the cause of Allah
  • The wayfarer (stranded traveler)

Sadaqah - Voluntary Charity

Beyond obligatory Zakat, Ramadan is an excellent time for Sadaqah (voluntary charity). The Prophet (peace be upon him) was the most generous of people, and he was even more generous during Ramadan.

  • Give to those in need without expecting anything in return
  • Provide iftar meals for fasting people
  • Support Islamic education and da'wah efforts
  • Help build or maintain mosques
  • Contribute to humanitarian causes
  • Remember: even a smile is charity

Zakat al-Fitr

Zakat al-Fitr is a special charity given before Eid prayer. It purifies the fast and provides food for the needy so they can celebrate Eid. Every Muslim, regardless of age or wealth, should give this charity (or have it given on their behalf).

Chapter 8

Eid al-Fitr - The Celebration

The Joy of Eid

Eid al-Fitr marks the end of Ramadan and is a day of celebration, gratitude, and joy. It's a gift from Allah to the believers who successfully completed the month of fasting. The word "Eid" means festivity, and "Fitr" means breaking the fast.

Eid Preparations and Etiquette

  • Take a bath (ghusl) and wear your best clothes
  • Apply perfume (for men)
  • Eat something sweet (like dates) before going to prayer
  • Pay Zakat al-Fitr before the Eid prayer
  • Go to the prayer ground early
  • Take different routes to and from the prayer (Sunnah)
  • Recite Takbeer: "Allahu Akbar, Allahu Akbar, La ilaha illallah, Wallahu Akbar, Allahu Akbar, Wa lillahil hamd"

The Eid Prayer

The Eid prayer consists of two rak'ahs with additional takbeers. It's followed by a khutbah (sermon). The prayer is a communal obligation and should be attended by all, including women and children.

Eid Celebrations

  • Visit family and friends, strengthening bonds
  • Exchange gifts, especially with children
  • Prepare special meals and sweets
  • Forgive others and seek forgiveness
  • Remember those who are less fortunate
  • Maintain the good habits developed during Ramadan
  • Express gratitude to Allah for enabling you to complete Ramadan

After Ramadan

While Ramadan has ended, the spiritual journey continues. Strive to maintain the good habits you developed:

  • Continue regular Qur'an recitation
  • Maintain night prayers (Qiyam)
  • Fast six days of Shawwal for additional reward
  • Keep giving charity regularly
  • Preserve the spiritual consciousness gained
  • Plan for the next Ramadan
